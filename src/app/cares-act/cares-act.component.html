CARES Act Details

What is the CARES Act?
The Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security Act (CARES Act) is a $2 trillion stimulus allocated for businesses, individuals, federal agencies, and state and local governments to assist in relieving stress and uncertainty being experienced during the coronavirus pandemic. The CARES Act is designed to distribute capital quickly and broadly, with many provisions to help small businesses.
Paycheck Protection Program
The Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) is a loan program that provides loan forgiveness for retaining employees. This expands the tradition SBA 7(a) loan program turning these loans to grants if funds are used towards payroll costs.
Economic Injury Disaster Loans Advances
The Economic Injury Disaster Loans (EIDLS) are loan advances that will provide up to $10,000 of economic relief to businesses that are experiencing difficulties in this crisis. It will provide economic relief to businesses currently experiencing a temporary loss of revenue. Funds will be made available within three (3) days of successful application according to the Small Business Association.
